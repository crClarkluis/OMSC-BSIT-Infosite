This program is designed to produce well-rounded IT professionals who are not only technically proficient but also possess critical thinking skills, adaptability, and a sense of responsibility in the ethical and societal dimensions of their work. The goal is to prepare graduates to excel in a variety of information technology-related professions and to make meaningful contributions to the field.
The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) is a comprehensive undergraduate degree program that focuses on providing students with a strong foundation in various aspects of information technology. This course is designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to navigate the dynamic and evolving field of technology.

Educational Outcomes for OMSC's Bachelor of Science in Information Technology Program

1.

Foundational Knowledge:

Graduates will demonstrate a comprehensive understanding of core foundational aspects of information technology, including programming, systems analysis, and network management.

2.

Problem Solving:

Graduates will be proficient in applying their technical acumen to analyze and solve business problems through the effective utilization of information technology tools and methodologies.Nam pretium turpis et arcu.

3.

Innovation and Emerging Technologies:

Graduates will exhibit the ability to innovate by staying abreast of new and emerging technologies, demonstrating adaptability and a proactive approach to incorporating these innovations into practical solutions.

4.

Global Competence:

Graduates will possess global competence, allowing them to navigate and contribute effectively in diverse international work environments, understanding the cultural, economic, and technological nuances of the global information technology landscape.

5.

Ethical and Social Responsibility:

Graduates will be socially and ethically responsible computing professionals, demonstrating a commitment to ethical practices, societal well-being, and the responsible use of technology in accordance with established standards and norms.

6.

Life-long Learning:

Graduates will engage in life-long learning endeavors, showcasing the ability to adapt to evolving technologies and industry trends, and actively seeking opportunities for professional development to stay current in the rapidly changing field of information technology.

Admission Requirements

Incoming Freshmen:

- Must have received a general high school average of at least 88%.
- Must have completed English, Math, and Science in high school with a grade of no less than 85%.
- Passed the screening for colleges

‍Transferee Students:

- Must have spent a minimum of one (1) year at a prior college or institution, without failing, incomplete, or discontinued courses.
- Passed the screening for colleges
- The program's acceptance of transferees' applications is contingent upon the availability of slots.

Graduation Requirements:

- A candidate for graduation must submit his graduation application to the Registrar's Office at the beginning of his final semester.
- When a student satisfies all academic and additional requirements set forth by the school, he will be recommended for graduation.
- A candidate for graduation must complete the necessary coursework and have their record cleared no later than two weeks prior to the conclusion of their final semester.
- A student cannot receive a diploma and transcript of records until all of his obligations have been fulfilled.

Program Information

Courses

For admission to the Bachelor of Science in Information Technology Program candidates for a technology degree program must meet English Competency plus one (1) of the other requirements:

- High school diploma from a university-recognized high school with a minimum 2.0 cumulative GPA or university-recognized high school equivalency such as GED, TASC, or HiSET

- Permission from the admissions committee after considering elements thought to be crucial for academic performance, such as prior academic success, extracurricular accomplishments, and any more data that the Committee has requested as they connect to the guidelines established by the University's governing bodies.

Concentration

Cloud Computing:
Gain a solid foundation in business cloud computing technologies, covering virtualization, storage, APIs, scripting, AWS, Azure, cloud security, disaster recovery, and strategic cloud implementation concepts.

Web Development:
Enroll in the FullStack Coding Bootcamp at Occidental Mindoro State College, providing a comprehensive path for students interested in dynamic web programming. Acquire front-end and back-end development skills to meet industry demands.

IT Project Management (ITPM):
Develop a strong foundation in IT project management through the Project Management Professional certification. Focus on integration, scope, time, cost, leadership, schedule, risk, quality, and communications management, with practical assignments for real-world application.

Cybersecurity:
Advance your career in information technology security with a comprehensive certificate program covering network security, cybersecurity fundamentals, cryptography, computer forensics, threat detection, information security management, and proactive cybersecurity design. Graduates can apply cybersecurity policies across various industries, including international business, healthcare, manufacturing, research and development, and banking and finance.
